What is NS2.0?

NS2.0 (National Strategy for Financial Literacy 2026–2030) is a national initiative introduced by the Financial Education Network (FEN) to strengthen financial literacy and financial resilience among Malaysians. Building upon the foundation of NS1.0, the strategy focuses on empowering individuals with the knowledge, skills, and confidence needed to make informed financial decisions in an increasingly digital and dynamic economic environment.


The initiative emphasizes several key priorities, including wise financial planning, responsible debt management, risk protection, safe usage of digital financial services, and wealth creation through informed investments. Through a whole-of-nation approach involving government agencies, financial institutions, educational bodies, and community organizations, NS2.0 aims to cultivate a financially literate society that is capable of achieving long-term financial well-being and sustainable economic growth.

Online Scam Awareness

Online scams are becoming more common in Malaysia. Scammers often use fake messages, fake banking websites and phone calls to steal money.

Do Not Share Passwords

Never share your bank password or OTP number with anyone.

Check Website Security

Always make sure the website starts with HTTPS before making payments.

Be Careful with Unknown Links

Avoid clicking suspicious links from SMS, email or social media.

Safe Online Banking

Online banking makes transactions easier and faster, but users must understand how to protect their accounts.

Strong Password

Use a strong password with numbers and symbols.

Secure Internet

Avoid using public WiFi when accessing online banking.

Transaction Alerts

Turn on banking notifications to monitor account activities.

Smart Cashless Budgeting

Cashless payments are convenient, but users should manage their expenses carefully.

Set Monthly Budget

Track your spending regularly using budgeting apps or notes.

Avoid Impulse Purchases

Think carefully before purchasing unnecessary items online.

Save for Emergencies

Prepare emergency savings for unexpected situations.
